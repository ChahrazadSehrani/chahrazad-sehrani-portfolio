# Research Agenda

## PhD research Vision

My research vision is to contribute to the development of trustworthy AI systems for industry, where autonomous decisions are not treated as pure optimization problems, but as complex socio-technical decisions involving humans, risks, responsibilities, and ethical trade-offs.

## Research Axes

### Axis 1 — Ethical Prompt Engineering for LLMs

Designing structured prompts that guide LLMs to reason about ethical trade-offs in high-stakes industrial scenarios.

### Axis 2 — Hybrid AI for Digital Twins

Combining neural reasoning from LLMs with symbolic and semantic representations of industrial systems.

### Axis 3 — Semantic Representation of Manufacturing Contexts

Representing the state of a Digital Twin through structured data formats that are understandable by both machines and LLMs.

### Axis 4 — Evaluation of Ethical Reasoning

Building evaluation protocols for LLM-based decision-making where there is no single obvious ground truth.

Possible evaluation dimensions:

- decision consistency,
- robustness to scenario perturbations,
- ethical alignment,
- context sensitivity,
- explanation quality,
- safety compliance,
- fairness of decisions,
- production impact.

### Axis 5 — Human-Centered Autonomous Systems

Designing AI systems that preserve human safety, well-being, and trust while supporting industrial performance.
