# PhD Thesis

## Ethical Digital Twins for Smart Manufacturing

**PhD Researcher:** Chahrazad SEHRANI  
**Affiliation:** CRAN – CNRS / Université de Lorraine  
**Supervisors:** Prof. Hind Bril El Haouzi and Prof. Yinling Liu  
**Period:** 2025 – 2028

## Research Summary

My PhD research focuses on developing ethical reasoning capabilities for Digital Twins in smart manufacturing.

The objective is to enable autonomous industrial systems to make decisions that are:

- explainable,
- human-centered,
- morally aware,
- safe,
- auditable,
- and trustworthy.

The research combines:

- Large Language Models,
- prompt engineering,
- semantic AI,
- structured state representation,
- digital twin architectures,
- and human–robot collaboration scenarios.

## Core Research Question

How can Large Language Models help Digital Twins reason ethically in high-stakes industrial environments?

## Application Context

The target context is smart manufacturing, especially scenarios involving:

- human operators,
- mobile robots or cobots,
- production objectives,
- fatigue constraints,
- safety constraints,
- quality constraints,
- and dynamic decision-making.

## Research Direction

The research investigates whether LLMs can support ethical decision-making when provided with structured industrial context and explicit or implicit ethical policies.

The long-term objective is not only to optimize industrial performance, but also to improve trust, transparency, and responsibility in autonomous industrial systems.
